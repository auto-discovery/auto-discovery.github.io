Quick story

The shape of the problem. Every TTT-Discover baseline commits to one root lineage by step ~10-20 and exploits it for the rest of training. The PUCT exploration term decays to zero faster than Q grows. The policy keeps producing diverse rollouts per step, but always around the same point in representation space.
What the methods did. Rep-novelty injects an independent exploration signal into selection — wins AC1 cleanly. QD-archive replaces the lineage buffer with a behavior-cell archive — wins erdos, narrowly. Neither helps AC2 (the "active unlearning" task).
The caveat we discovered later. The whole diagnostic was at 64 rollouts/step. At paper-spec 512 rollouts/step (Phase A, currently running), the bare baseline reaches 0.380965 on erdos — better than every method we tested at 64 rollouts. The "method wins" may have been measuring a weaker baseline, not a stronger method. The H1-H4 mechanisms are still real per-step phenomena; their *practical cost* may shrink at proper batch.
